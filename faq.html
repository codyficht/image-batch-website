Frequently Asked Questions

What is Image-Batch?

Image-Batch is a powerful Mac app designed for bulk image editing. With it, you can resize, crop, compress, convert, watermark, rename, rotate, flip, and find duplicate images with ease.

Can I resize a bunch of images at once on Mac?

Yes. Image-Batch is built for batch resizing on Mac — select a folder (or individual photos), set width, height, or largest side, and process the whole batch at once while keeping aspect ratios. Free Lite handles up to 3 images; Pro up to 40; Premium is unlimited.

Can I crop hundreds of images to the same size on Mac?

Yes. Use Crop by Size to set exact pixel dimensions once and apply that same crop to an entire batch — ideal when you need every photo the same size for web, print, or product listings. Crop by Aspect Ratio is also available for social formats like Instagram or YouTube.

How do I batch compress photos on Mac without uploading them?

Image-Batch compresses images entirely on your Mac — no cloud upload. Reduce by percentage for quick file-size savings, or (Premium) set a target max file size per image for strict upload limits. All processing stays private and local.

Can I convert HEIC to JPEG in bulk on Mac?

Yes. Use Convert to turn HEIC/HEIF (and other formats) into JPEG, PNG, TIFF, AVIF, or WEBP in one batch — with adjustable quality. Everything runs offline on your Mac, so photos never leave your computer. Extended formats depend on your tier (see Pricing).

Is there a free batch photo editor for Mac?

Yes. Image-Batch Lite is free on the Mac App Store and includes core bulk tools (resize, crop, compress, convert to JPEG, watermark, rename, and find duplicates) for up to 3 images per batch. Upgrade to Pro or Premium anytime with a one-time purchase — no subscription.

Can I watermark multiple photos at once on Mac?

Yes. Add a custom watermark image to an entire batch in seconds. Pro and Premium unlock custom watermark positioning for more control over placement.

Does Image-Batch upload my photos or need the internet?

No. Image-Batch processes images 100% locally on your Mac. Your photos are not uploaded to our servers. You only need the internet to download the app or complete in-app purchases — not to edit images.

How does Image-Batch work?

You simply select a folder of images you want to process, choose from Image-Batch's eight versatile features, adjust the options according to your needs, and the app does the rest for you.

What types of image formats does Image-Batch support?

Image-Batch supports a wide range of formats including WEBP, AVIF, JPEG/JPG, PNG, HEIF/HEIC, and TIFF.

How much does Image-Batch cost?

Image-Batch offers flexible pricing tiers to suit your needs:

  • Free Version (Lite): Access to core features with a limit of 3 images per batch.
  • Pro Version ($8.99 one-time): Extended functionality for heavier use — up to 40 images per batch, custom output folders, Direct Edit Mode, extended formats (PNG, HEIF, TIFF), custom crop & watermark positioning, and more.
  • Premium Version ($19.99 one-time): The complete professional toolkit — unlimited images, reduce to exact file size, precision crop by pixels, largest-side resize, AVIF & WEBP conversion, full rotate & flip, and everything in Pro.

Users who previously purchased Image-Batch at a one-time price are automatically upgraded to the Premium version upon updating. Where can I get support for Image-Batch?

You can reach out to our support team through the contact form on our website. We're always here to help!

Can I suggest a new feature for Image-Batch?

Absolutely! We're always open to user feedback and suggestions. Feel free to contact us through our website's contact form.

Can I edit images directly in their original folder, or will Image-Batch create a new folder with edited images?

With Image-Batch, you have full control. If you're on the Pro or Premium tier, you can use "Direct Edit Mode" to save changes directly to the original folder (when using folder selection). If you prefer to keep originals untouched, Image-Batch creates a new output folder for edited images.

How does the 'Bulk Reduction' feature work?

Image-Batch's 'Reduce' feature decreases file size through compression (for JPEG, HEIF, WEBP) or scaling (for all formats). For maximum reduction, the app may apply both. Note that heavy compression can affect image quality — always test results for your use case.

What is the maximum amount of images that Image-Batch can handle at once?

Image-Batch offers different processing capabilities based on your tier:

  • Lite Version: Up to 3 images at once.
  • Pro Upgrade: Up to 40 images at once.
  • Premium Upgrade: Unlimited image processing (tested with 500+ images).

Performance depends on image size, your Mac's specs, and the specific edits being performed.

How does Image-Batch handle folder selection and sub-folders?

When you select a folder, Image-Batch only processes image files directly in that folder. Sub-folders are not accessed or modified — giving you precise control over which images are edited.

How do I edit a batch of photos from the Apple Photos app?

Create a new folder on your Mac, drag the photos from Apple Photos into it, then select that folder in Image-Batch. After editing, you can drag the output images back into Apple Photos if desired.

Can I perform multiple operations like bulk resizing and renaming at the same time?

Image-Batch performs one operation at a time for precision and safety. After completing one process (e.g., resizing), you can immediately run the next (e.g., renaming) on the same or new batch.

On which operating systems is Image-Batch available?

Image-Batch is exclusively available for macOS 15.0 and higher. We do not currently offer versions for other operating systems.

Why aren't some images in my selected folder being edited?

Images not fully downloaded from iCloud cannot be edited. Check each image in the folder and click the cloud icon to download it before starting your batch edit.

What is the Preferred File Extensions setting?

This setting lets you choose whether Image-Batch uses Apple's preferred extensions (e.g., .jpeg instead of .jpg). Toggle it in Settings to maintain consistent file naming across your library.

What is the Default Export Compression Quality setting?

This controls the default compression level for JPEG, WebP, HEIF, and AVIF exports. You can set your preferred quality in Settings — it applies automatically to all compressible formats. Individual Convert operations can override this with their own slider.

Why does processing AVIF files take longer?

AVIF uses advanced compression that delivers superior quality at smaller file sizes, but it requires more processing power. Please be patient when working with AVIF files — the results are worth it!

What is Largest Side Resizing?

Largest Side Resizing (Premium only) automatically detects the longest dimension of each image and resizes that side to your specified value while preserving aspect ratio. Perfect for fitting images into specific size requirements regardless of orientation.
